The fire prevention plan shall as a minimum specify the:

  • total amount of waste (tonnes and cubic metres) that will be stored on-site at any one time and, if more than one type of waste is to be stored on site, the total amount of each type of waste
  • maximum time each type of waste will be stored on site
  • method of storage of each type of waste
  • maximum volume of each waste pile in cubic metres and the number of piles
  • location within the site where each type of waste will be stored
  • maximum size of any waste pile stack, stipulating the maximum height, width and depth in metres;
  • minimum separation (fire break) distance between waste piles or storage areas
  • full details of the design and construction of fire walls, if fire walls are used in place of fire breaks
  • steps put in place to prevent and minimise the risk of a fire or of its spreading within the site or from the site
  • steps put in place to extinguish a fire if one starts
  • steps and procedures to be followed if a fire occurs on site, including how the impact or emissions from a fire that may affect people or the environment will be minimised
  • provisions made to enable safe access to the site for emergency responders, and how the impact on people and the environment from fighting the fire will be minimised

There will be other aspects you will have to cover in your fire prevention plan for example the type of operation, the scale of it and the location.
